📌 About Us

Human Rights Violation and Advocacy Foundation (HRVF) is a non-profit, non-governmental organization founded in 2025 in Delta State, Nigeria. Our mission is to protect, uphold, and spread awareness about fundamental human rights enshrined in the Nigerian Constitution and international law.

HRVF is a group of activists, lawyers, doctors, pastors, and community leaders dedicated to challenging oppression, exploitation, and discrimination at all levels of society. We collaborate with other human rights groups to organize programs and movements for the protection of marginalized communities.

🌍 Vision

To build a just society where every individual enjoys fundamental rights and freedoms without discrimination.

🎯 Objectives

  1. Prevent human rights violations and safeguard fundamental freedoms.
  2. Promote community mobilization and social development.
  3. Conduct research and publish educational materials on rights and justice.
  4. Advocate through media, seminars, and awareness campaigns.
  5. Expose abuses by state agencies and demand accountability.
  6. Provide legal aid to the poor and marginalized.
  7. Collaborate with human rights commissions, NGOs, and media.
  8. Fight against child labour, trafficking, and gender violence.
  9. Support the physically challenged with training and facilities.
  10. Organize youth programs to prevent drug abuse, cultism, and vices.
  11. Promote peace, national integration, and communal harmony.

👤 Chairman’s Message

“HRVF is committed to being a voice for the voiceless. Together, we must build a society where dignity, equality, and justice prevail for all Nigerians.”

📜 Constitutional Rights

The Constitution of the Federal Republic of Nigeria guarantees fundamental rights, including:

  • Right to life
  • Right to dignity of human person
  • Right to personal liberty
  • Right to fair hearing
  • Right to private and family life
  • Right to freedom of thought, conscience, and religion
  • Right to freedom of expression and the press
  • Right to peaceful assembly and association
  • Right to freedom of movement
  • Right to freedom from discrimination
